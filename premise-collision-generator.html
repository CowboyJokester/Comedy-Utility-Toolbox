Premise Collision Generator

Smash two unrelated ideas together and see if comedy falls out. If not, blame the second idea.

Need help smashing two ideas into one comedy premise?

How to Use This Tool

Purpose

The Premise Collision Generator combines two unrelated ideas to create unexpected comedy premises, unusual comparisons, and fresh joke setups.

When To Use It

Use this tool when you need a new premise, when your writing feels predictable, or when you want to force two worlds together and see where the conflict, confusion, or absurdity appears.

How To Use It

  1. Enter the first idea.
  2. Enter the second idea.
  3. Choose a comedy tone.
  4. Click Generate Premises.
  5. Look for conflict, confusion, frustration, misunderstanding, or absurdity.
  6. Use the strongest collision as a joke, story, bit, sketch, or writing prompt.

Example Input

First Idea: Grandpa
Second Idea: Self-checkout
Tone: Rural / Old-Timer

Example Output

Collision Angle: What happens when Grandpa treats a self-checkout machine like a lazy cashier who needs discipline?

Comedy Tip

Do not hunt for punchlines first. Hunt for conflict. Conflict is the old tractor engine; punchlines are what happens once it finally fires up.
