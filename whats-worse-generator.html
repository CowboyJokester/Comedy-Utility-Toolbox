What's Worse? Generator

Take a simple annoyance, problem, or situation and push it through stronger comparison levels until the comedy pressure starts talking.
Need help making a premise worse, sharper, and funnier?

How to Use This Tool

Purpose

The What's Worse? Generator helps comedians take a simple problem, annoyance, character trait, or premise and push it through stronger comparison levels. It is designed to help you find sharper punchline directions, bigger act-outs, stronger tags, and clearer final comedy images.

When To Use It

Use this tool when a premise feels too small, too plain, or too obvious. It is especially useful when you know something is funny, but you need to make the situation worse, stranger, more embarrassing, more specific, or more visual before it becomes stage-ready.

How To Use It

  1. Enter a problem, annoyance, character trait, or comedy premise.
  2. Choose the category that best fits the idea.
  3. Choose the What's Worse style.
  4. Choose the comedy tone for your audience or venue.
  5. Click Generate What's Worse Ladder.
  6. Use the ladder to find stronger comparisons, exaggerations, tags, and final images.

Example Input

Premise:
Grandpa bought a smartphone.

Category:
Technology

Style:
Character Flaw Worse

Example Output

The tool may guide you from the simple problem — Grandpa does not understand the phone — toward stronger levels: he argues with the screen, blames the phone for spying, calls the phone company like it is a sheriff's department, and finally treats the flashlight app like government surveillance.

Comedy Tip

The funniest answer is not always the wildest answer. Look for the comparison that is worse, clearer, and more specific. Specific beats general every time.
